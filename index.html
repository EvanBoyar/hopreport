Notes on method

Each band's score is the product of four gates, so a single bad factor is enough to close a band: score = 100 × MUF × absorption × geomagnetic × season. The MUF gate uses the nearest fresh ionosonde's measured MUF(3000) when one is reachable through prop.kc2g.com, and otherwise falls back to an estimate built from SFI. A band scores full marks below roughly 0.85 of the MUF and falls off sharply above it. The absorption gate models D-layer loss from the sun's elevation at your grid, scaled by 1/f² and multiplied by the current GOES X-ray flare class, which is what closes 160 through 40 m in daylight and opens them at night. The geomagnetic gate lowers the MUF by 5% for each Kp point above 3 and applies a direct penalty scaled to your latitude, weighted toward the high bands. The season gate trims the low bands for summer static. Treat the 6 m figure as a floor rather than a forecast, since nothing in the indices predicts sporadic E. The LUF readout is derived from the same absorption gate rather than modeled separately: it is the frequency where daytime D-layer loss alone would pull a band below fair. It is shown for orientation and never feeds the scores a second time, since each band's absorption factor already contains it. A real LUF also depends on your power, antenna, and mode. Weak-signal modes such as FT8 dig well below the figure shown, which is why live spots can appear under it. When the LUF climbs past the MUF during a strong flare, the page flags a shortwave fadeout.

The live layer subscribes to PSK Reporter's public MQTT feed over WebSockets, filtered to spots transmitted from or received in your grid square and its eight neighbors, kept in a rolling 15 minute window. Once a band collects three or more spots, its displayed score becomes 0.6 live + 0.4 model, where the live score combines spot count with maximum distance normalized for each band. Measured reality outvotes the prediction in both directions. CW spots arrive through the same feed because PSK Reporter ingests the Reverse Beacon Network's skimmer spots, and the CW only switch rescores the live layer from those alone. The feed skews toward FT8 monitors, so a band that is lively on SSB but quiet on digital and CW can still read low. The callsign query folds your own reception reports in through the retrieval API, limited to one query every five minutes under PSK Reporter's rules. If every source fails at once, the browser or network is blocking cross-origin requests; save this file and open it directly.
